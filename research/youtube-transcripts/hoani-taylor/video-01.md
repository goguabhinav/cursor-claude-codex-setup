## Key Takeaways

- Volume beats everything. You can't write copy 3x better. You can send 3x more.
- The email doesn't close. It starts. Cold reply = 3% trust. The sale happens in the nurture process most people skip.
- Specificity is the only trust signal you have. Vague copy fails not because it's bad writing but because it's not believable.
- The list is the hidden variable. Better coverage = harder-to-find emails = less competition in the inbox.
- Soft CTA gets 5x more replies and equal or better calls. "Can I send a video?" beats "open to a quick chat?" almost every time.

- Tone beats script. Genuineness is the variable.

## Copy Principles

**Two jobs every email must do:**
1. Build trust
2. Entice with something of value (outcome or lead magnet)

**Specificity = trust.** "We help plumbing companies get more water heater installation leads from people getting their house ready for sale by connecting them with real estate agents in Austin, TX" is orders of magnitude more believable than "we generate leads."

**The prospect doesn't care what you do. Only what they get.** Stop explaining the service.

**Don't criticize prospects.** Reframe: don't say "your website needs work," say "I drafted a version of your site you might find interesting — want me to send it?"

**Subject lines:** `question [first name]` wins against almost everything. Lowercase performs better than title case.

**Spam to avoid:** Links in first email, "free," "guarantee," exclamation marks, identical email bodies.

## Call-to-Action Selection

| CTA | Positive reply rate (rough) | Quality |
|---|---|---|
| "Open to a quick chat?" | Baseline | Mixed |
| "Can I send over a quick video?" | ~5x the above | Higher quality calls |
| "Point me in the right direction?" | Highest volume | Requires more nurture |

**Industry CTA matching:**
- Blue collar → phone call CTA
- White collar / digital / ecom → video CTA

**Never send a calendar link to a cold prospect.** Propose times. 40-50% conversion advantage vs. links.

## Lead Nurture Sequence

**What actually moves the ball after a reply:**
1. Add on LinkedIn immediately
2. Reply with a personal Loom video answering their question
3. Propose two specific times (don't send a link)
4. If no reply → call at the first proposed time
5. If no answer → text + email follow-up
6. Every 12 weeks → block 2-3 days and make personalized Loom videos for every positive reply that didn't close

**The 12-week re-Loom:** If you have 120 leads that went warm but didn't book, you already paid to generate them. Making 120 personal Looms in 72 hours is high ROI with zero additional acquisition cost.

## Personalized Loom Video System

**Two structures:**
- **Curiosity structure** (scalable): Record once, overlay on each prospect's website. "Hey, I have something I think could be an opportunity for you — can I show you?"
- **Show value structure** (better, harder to scale): Walk through a live demo, show real results, explain exactly what you'd do for them. 3-6 minutes.

**Thumbnail hack:** Record your video while showing the prospect's website in the background. When the Loom GIF preview lands in their inbox, they see their own site on screen. Curiosity spikes.

**Loom view rate from people who requested it:** ~60%. Of those, 10-20% book a call.

## Diagnosing Underperforming Campaigns

**Three failure types:**
1. Deliverability issue
2. Lead list issue (wrong people)
3. Copy issue (right people, wrong message)

**Signs it's copy:** Lots of "not interested" / "unsubscribe" replies, very few real human responses.

**Sanity test for lead lists:** Open 30 random domains. Would you sell to this company if they said yes? If fewer than 80% = yes, your list is the problem.
