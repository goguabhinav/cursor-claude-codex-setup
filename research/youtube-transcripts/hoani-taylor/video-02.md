# Cold Email Infrastructure: Cheap Inboxes at Scale

**Source:** Carrots Not Sticks (Hani Taylor) — Azure/Intra inbox deep dive

---

## Core Argument

The dirty secret of cold email (per Nick Abraham, Leadbird): **you just want to send more for less.**

Copy fads come and go. Video outreach, spin tax, warming pools — all of it fluctuates. What doesn't change is the math: lower cost per inbox = more volume = more leads. Cold email scales roughly linearly. If something works at 10,000 emails, it works at 100,000. Most people don't scale because the infrastructure cost makes it feel risky. Solve that and the problem mostly solves itself.

---

## The Cost Problem

**Traditional reseller pricing:** ~$3/inbox/month (Google or Outlook via Zap Mail, Premium Inboxes, Mail Reef, etc.)

At 20 emails/day per inbox, sending 500,000 emails/month requires ~2,500 inboxes.

| Method | Cost for 500k emails/month |
|---|---|
| Traditional resellers | ~$2,500 |
| Azure/Intra method | ~$166–$500 |

That's 5x to 15x cheaper for the same volume. Or put differently: for the same budget, you send 15x more emails. No copy improvement can compete with that multiplier.

**Real-world example from the video:** Their biggest client is sending 200,000 emails/day (6 million/month), emailing 100,000 net new leads/day. At 1% reply rate with 60% positive replies, that's **600 leads/day.** That's only possible because the infrastructure cost is low enough to justify the volume.

## What "Azure Inboxes" Actually Are

Not a real Microsoft product category — it's a marketing term cold emailers invented to describe a loophole in Microsoft's licensing.

**How it works (simplified):**
1. Buy a cheap Microsoft Exchange license (basic business product, ~one mailbox)
2. With that license you get a **tenant**
3. Use Microsoft Azure (their cloud) to spin up ~100 inboxes on that tenant
4. These inboxes can send and receive email — no UI, no app, just cloud-based sending
5. Attach those inboxes to your sequencer (SmartLead, Instantly, etc.)

**Common reseller setup (e.g. Hypertide):** 2 domains per tenant, 50 inboxes per domain, 2 emails/day per inbox.

**The SaaS tool approach:** Instead of buying from a reseller who does this on their backend, you can use a tool (referenced as "our engine" in the video) that lets you:
- Add your own tenants
- Buy your own domains
- Spin up 100 inboxes yourself
- Upload directly to your sequencer

This cuts out the reseller margin entirely.

---

## Why This Probably Won't Last

Microsoft almost certainly didn't intend for Exchange licenses to be used this way. The moment enough people abuse it, Microsoft patches the loophole. Everyone in the space knows this and is treating it as a time-limited arbitrage.

When it closes: you go back to normal Google/Microsoft inboxes at standard pricing. Not the end of the world — just more expensive. The window where you can send 15x the volume for the same cost is finite.

**Recommended mindset:** Make hay while the sun shines. 10 leads today beats 1 lead/week for 10 weeks. Get reps, build clients, grow faster now. Don't wait out of fear of the loophole closing.

## Key Takeaways

- Infrastructure cost is a multiplier on everything else. Get it low and volume becomes your main lever.
- Most resellers selling "Azure" or "Intra" inboxes are doing this same thing on their own backend — you're just paying their margin on top.
- The SaaS tool approach lets you do it yourself for cheaper, but you need to source your own tenants.
- This is a loophole. Treat it like one. Use it aggressively while it exists.
- When it closes, fall back to standard resellers (Google/Outlook). Still works, just at standard pricing.
