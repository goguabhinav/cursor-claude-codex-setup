## Key Takeaways

1. **Warm calling is 10x more effective than cold calling.** 1 meeting per 30 dials (warm) vs 1 per 300 dials (cold).
2. **Calling interested email replies is the single highest-ROI action in outbound.** People who replied positively but didn't book are in your inbox right now.
3. **Phone numbers are often in the signature.** 20–50% of interested email replies include a phone number already.
4. **Only call mobile numbers.** Landlines = gatekeepers = wasted time.
5. **The warm call opener is almost unfair.** "Hey, we exchanged an email about X — does that ring a bell?" creates implicit obligation.

## Cold vs Warm Calling

| Type | Meeting per dial ratio |
|---|---|
| Cold calling | ~1 per 300 dials |
| Warm calling | ~1 per 30 dials |

**Real example:** SmartLead team called a backlog of interested email replies in one day. Result: 10 meetings booked in one day.

## Script Framework

### Cold Call Script

**Opener:** Use their first name directly. Don't ask "Is this Nancy?" — dead giveaway it's a cold call.

> "Nancy — hey, this is Matt calling from Anivvo. Giving you a quick call because I noticed you run a Shopify store and your site load speed was a little longer than I'd expect. Do you have a couple minutes to chat?"

**Close:** Push for the meeting. Be direct. Propose times. Most people fail here by softening the ask.

---

### Warm Call Script

**Opener:** Reference the email exchange immediately.

> "Susie — hey, this is Matt calling from Anivvo. Giving you a quick call because we exchanged an email about cold outreach and it sounded like you were interested in generating more meetings. Does that ring a bell?"

Even if they say no: "Totally, I think we chatted about X — basically what we do is..." Then pitch and push for a call.

**Why this works:** Creates implicit obligation (they said yes over email — hanging up now is awkward), and they're already pre-qualified.

---

### Objection Handling

**Play dumb:** If the SDR can't answer a complex question:

> "Honestly, I just started here and I don't know the answer to that — but I'd love to get you in with [founder/expert]. They'll be able to answer all your questions."

Takes pressure off, makes the call feel less like a pitch, still gets the meeting.

## Integration with Cold Email

1. Cold email campaign goes out via SmartLead
2. Interested replies come into master inbox
3. Check signature for phone number (free)
4. If no number → enrich via Prospeo/Clay waterfall
5. Filter for mobile numbers only
6. Warm call using opener: "we exchanged an email about X"
7. Book the meeting on the call or propose times

The email does the prospecting. The call does the converting.
