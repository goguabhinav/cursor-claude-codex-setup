## Key Takeaways

1. **Warm calling is 10x more effective than cold calling.** 1 meeting per 30 dials (warm) vs roughly 1 per 300 dials (cold). Not a rounding error — a completely different game.
2. **Calling interested email replies is the single highest-ROI action in outbound.** People who responded positively to your cold email but didn't book a call are sitting in your inbox right now. Call them. You'll convert a chunk of them in hours, not days.
3. **Phone numbers are often already in the signature.** 20–50% of interested email replies include a phone number. You don't need a data tool for those.
4. **Only call mobile numbers.** Use a validation tool to filter out landlines and VoIP before dialing. Landlines = gatekeepers = wasted time.
5. **The warm call opener is almost unfair.** "Hey, we exchanged an email about X — does that ring a bell?" creates an implicit obligation. They already expressed interest. Hanging up now is awkward for them.
6. **Cold calling ratio mirrors cold email ratio at ~10x.** If it takes 3,000 emails to book one meeting, expect ~300 cold dials. Use this to set team expectations.
7. **You don't need an expert. You need someone who can smile and take rejection.** SDRs are a trainable hire. Attitude and coachability beat experience every time.

## Cold vs Warm Calling (The Core Distinction)

| Type | What it is | Meeting per dial ratio | Pickup rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cold calling | Calling strangers, fully unsolicited | ~1 per 300 dials | ~10% |
| Warm calling | Calling people who expressed email interest | ~1 per 30 dials | Higher (exact % varies) |

**Warm calling is the money play.** Cold email generates the leads. Warm calling converts them. The combination produces numbers that neither channel achieves alone.

**Real example:** SmartLead team had a backlog of interested email replies. Called them all in one day saying "Hey, you said you were interested in SmartLead — want to get on a call?" Result: 10 meetings booked in one day.

## Hiring SDRs

**What you're actually looking for:**
- Clear English, neutral accent, able to hold a natural conversation
- Extroverted energy (if they're scared to record a 2-minute Loom video, they can't call a $10M CEO)
- Hunger and drive over experience — this is a highly trainable skill
- Resilience and coachability — they will get hung up on, repeatedly

**Loom video test:** Ask every applicant to record a short video of themselves. Best pre-screen tool available. Filters out people who freeze on camera before you waste an interview slot on them.

**Hiring funnel:**
1. Post on LinkedIn Jobs ($5–15/day spend)
2. Show comp range upfront — more applicants
3. Hire from anywhere with good English (US, UK, Canada, South Africa, Latam, Asia)
4. Screen 100+ applicants via Loom
5. Filter on English quality, internet quality, energy
6. Technical phone assessment — test objection handling live
7. Hire 1–3 (plan for churn — this is a high-turnover role by industry standard)

**Tip:** Pay slightly above industry standard. Gets better candidates, reduces churn.

## Script Framework

### Cold Call Script

**Opener:** Use their first name directly, don't ask "Is this Nancy?" (dead giveaway it's a cold call)

> "Nancy — hey, this is Matt calling from Anivvo. Giving you a quick call because I noticed you run a Shopify store and your site load speed was a little longer than I'd expect. Do you have a couple minutes to chat?"

**Pitch:** Same as cold email — short, direct value prop. What you do, what result they get, reason to book a call.

**Close:** Push for the meeting repeatedly. Most people fail here by softening the ask. Be direct. Propose times.

---

### Warm Call Script

**Opener:** Reference the email exchange immediately.

> "Susie — hey, this is Matt calling from Anivvo. Giving you a quick call because we exchanged an email about cold outreach and it sounded like you were interested in generating more meetings. Does that ring a bell?"

Even if they say no, you still have an easy lead-in: "Totally, I think we chatted about X — basically what we do is..." Then go straight into pitch and push for a call.

**Why this works:**
- Creates implicit obligation (they said yes over email — hanging up now is awkward)
- They're already pre-qualified — you know they're interested
- Reduces the cold call friction almost entirely

---

### Objection Handling

**Play dumb:** If someone asks a complex question your SDR can't answer, don't fake it.

> "Honestly, I just started here and I don't know the answer to that — but I'd love to get you in with [founder/expert]. They'll be able to answer all your questions."

Takes pressure off the SDR, makes the call feel less like a pitch, and still gets the meeting.

## Sourcing Phone Numbers

**Priority order:**

1. **Email signature** — 20–50% of interested replies already have a phone number. Free. No tool needed.
2. **Lead Magic** — affordable mobile numbers
3. **Prospeo** — slightly more expensive, solid data quality (Matt's primary source)
4. **Apollo** — has phone numbers, widely available
5. **Clay waterfall** — runs multiple providers in sequence, maximizes coverage
6. **Find Email, Datagma, Contact Out** — other options in the waterfall

**Validation tool:** Clearout Phone (or similar) — upload a list, it tells you which numbers are mobile vs landline vs VoIP. Only dial mobile. Landlines reach gatekeepers. Mobile numbers reach the decision maker directly.

**Advanced:** TitanX (and similar tools) go one step further — they score phone numbers on likelihood to pick up, claiming 25%+ pickup rates. More expensive (~$0.50–$1/validation), but worth testing at scale.

SmartLead added a native "Call Lead" button in the master inbox. If someone has a number in their signature, copy it in, press go. No need to jump platforms. Best option for warm calling integrated with cold email workflow.

**For cold calling (standalone dialer):**

| Tool | Notes |
|---|---|
| Salesfinity | Matt's current choice. No lock-in, good features, mid-price |
| Trellis | Cheaper |
| Kixie / Aloware | More features, higher price |
| Nooks / Orum | Parallel dialing, higher price, 3-seat minimum + annual commitment |

**Power dialing vs parallel dialing:**  
Matt recommends power dialing (sequential). Parallel dialing (calling multiple people simultaneously) creates a delay when someone picks up — the awkward pause that signals "this is a bot." Also burns phone numbers faster.

**Volume benchmarks:**
- Power dialer: 50+ dials/hour
- Target for cold calling SDR: 100+ dials/day
- Warm calling (more research per lead): lower volume, higher conversion

## Training Process

1. Write the script (Google Doc is fine)
2. Practice out loud — slow down, watch tone and pacing
3. Record yourself — listen back
4. Practice on AI coaching bot (Salesfinity has one built in; other tools exist)
5. Mock calls with a teammate — one person dials the other, treat it like a real call
6. Go live by day 3–4 (you're never 100% ready, just start)

Skills needed are trainable. The job is hard psychologically, not technically. Attitude and coachability matter more than any specific skill.

## Integration with Cold Email (The Full Stack)

This is the actual system:

1. Cold email campaign goes out via SmartLead
2. Interested replies come into master inbox
3. Check signature for phone number (free)
4. If no number → enrich via Prospeo/Clay waterfall
5. Filter for mobile numbers only (Clearout Phone)
6. Warm call from SmartLead's native dial button (or Salesfinity)
7. Use warm call opener ("we exchanged an email about X")
8. Book the meeting on the call or propose times

The email does the prospecting. The call does the converting. Neither alone produces the same results as both together.

## Common Mistakes

- Calling landlines or company main lines — you get gatekeepers, not decision makers
- Asking "Is this [name]?" — immediately signals cold call, people hang up
- SDRs trying to answer every objection instead of playing dumb and booking a call with the expert
- Not calling interested email replies — biggest missed opportunity in most cold email operations
- Expecting cold call results without warm leads — it works but is 10x harder
- Waiting until the script is perfect before going live — go live by day 3-4, you learn faster on real calls
