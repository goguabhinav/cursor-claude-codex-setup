## Key Takeaways

1. **Volume is the lever. Everything else is a multiplier on it.** Only 3% of any market is actively buying at any given moment.
2. **The bridge model.** Offer → Lead list → Copy → Deliverability → Response handling. Miss one and the whole thing falls apart.
3. **Most meetings come from follow-up, not email one.** 50–60% of booked calls come from follow-up emails.
4. **Offer resonance is the biggest variable nobody talks about.** Same list, same copy, same infrastructure — a strong offer gets 1 meeting per 20 contacts. A weak offer gets 1 per 5,000.
5. **Appointment setting is where most people leak money.** Someone has to be in the inbox every day responding fast.

## Copy Framework

### One-to-One Principle

Write like you're talking to one person. Use "you" more than "I" and "we."

### Four-Part Email Structure

1. **Personalized opener** — why you're reaching out, specific to them
2. **Value prop** — what you do and what they get (2 sentences max)
3. **Case study** — proof it works for someone like them (optional)
4. **Call to action** — one clear next step

**Total length:** 4–5 sentences. Read in under 20 seconds.

### Template Examples

**Standard:**
> Hey [name], we help [industry] get [result] using [mechanism]. We just did this for [client] and they got [outcome] in [timeframe]. Interested in learning more?

**One-liner:**
> Hey [name], would you be interested in [specific outcome]?

**Case study lead:**
> Hey [name], was searching for [industry] companies and came across your site. We just helped another [industry] get [result] in [timeframe] — wondering if we could do something similar for [company].

**Video strategy:**
> Hey [name], I was browsing your site and had a couple ideas on how you could [fix pain point]. I just recorded a quick video. Is it okay if I send it over?

### Subject Lines

**What works:**
- `question, [first name]`
- `[first name]?`
- `thoughts`
- `quick question`
- `[relevant one-word topic]`

Short, vague, looks like it could be from a coworker. Don't write the value prop in the subject line.

### Spam Triggers to Avoid
- Links in email one
- "Free," "guarantee," exclamation marks
- All caps, giant text blocks, identical emails with no variation

## CTA Selection

| CTA | When to use |
|---|---|
| "Are you free Tuesday or Thursday?" | Strong offer, warm-ish audience |
| "Can I give you a quick 5-minute call?" | Blue collar, phone-friendly industries |
| "Want more info?" | Simple, no commitment, works universally |
| "Can I send you a free [deliverable]?" | Best overall — free sample drives interest AND pre-sells the service |

**Avoid:** Loom audit CTAs. High effort, 5–10% convert to calls, and the audit often doesn't tie directly to your service.

## Follow-Up Strategy

**For cold contacts:** Most meetings come from email 1, then email 2, then email 3. Diminishing returns kick in hard after 3–4.

**For interested leads:** Follow up indefinitely until they buy or explicitly say no.

**Three-channel follow-up sequence:**
1. Double dial (bypasses iPhone Do Not Disturb)
2. Email reply (answer question → tie to call → propose two times)
3. LinkedIn connection request

**Response framework:**
1. Answer their question
2. Tie their question to a reason they should get on a call
3. Propose two specific times in their timezone

## Offer Development

Do this before anything else.

**How to lower risk for the prospect:**
- Guarantee results
- Work on performance/results basis
- Offer a pilot (30 or 60 days)
- Offer a free deliverable to start

**Offer resonance scale:**
- Very strong (1.0) → 1 meeting per 20 contacts
- Average (0.1) → 1 meeting per 200 contacts
- Weak (0.01) → 1 meeting per 2,000 contacts
