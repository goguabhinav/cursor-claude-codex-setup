RESEARCH NOTES
————————————————————

Blueprint CTA:

"You commented on my LinkedIn post to get the cloud-powered prospecting system. It's the exact setup we used to book 100+ meetings in a few weeks.

Just wanted to check, did you receive it?

If not, I can resend.

Just reply yes."

Key lesson:
Instead of asking for a demo, ask for permission to send something valuable.

Winning email pattern from the teardown:

Prospect story
Specific research
Business problem
Solution

Example structure:

"Started at your parents' dining table → grew to 2,000+ retail stores."

Then connect that growth stage to the problem you solve.

The prospect becomes the main character of the email instead of the product.

Strong differentiation pattern:

Bad:
"We're better than Shopify Capital."

Good:
"While Shopify Capital gives fixed daily deductions, we let you design the draw around specific campaigns."

Specific mechanism > generic superiority claim.

————————————————————

KEY TAKEAWAYS
————————————————————

Replace demo CTAs with low-friction value CTAs.

"Reply yes" is a stronger first ask than "book 30 minutes."

Make the prospect the hero of the email.

Use a story → problem → solution sequence.

Differentiate on mechanism, not outcomes.

Ignore 80% of the article discussing infrastructure, volume, lead sourcing, and campaign operations.
